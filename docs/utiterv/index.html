Two RV Routes From Salt Lake City to Los Angeles

The stronger plan drops weak detours and protects the big experiences: Grand Canyon, Route 66, Las Vegas for half a day, desert scenery, and one California big-tree finish.

Big Picture Verdict

Best route: Bonneville only if it feels emotionally worth the first-day detour. After that, go south through Nevada/Arizona, not across every possible park.

Least sensible stops: Redwood National Park is too far north, Chiricahua is too far southeast, Robbers Roost conflicts with the no-dirt rule, and a true Grand Canyon bottom hike is unsafe without an overnight permit.

Keep: Route 66 Seligman-Kingman-Oatman-Topock, Grand Canyon South Rim, Lees Ferry if you want easy Colorado River access, and either Sequoia or Yosemite for giant trees.

Option A: Recommended

Balanced Canyon, Route 66, Sequoia

This is the best common-sense itinerary. It avoids Yosemite/Tioga uncertainty, uses Sequoia for giant trees, and keeps the route coherent for an RV.

Drop Without Regret

Redwood National Park, Chiricahua, Robbers Roost, and any one-day rim-to-river Grand Canyon plan.

Conditional Keep

Bonneville is worth keeping only if the idea excites you. It is a visual one-hour stop plus a routing penalty.

Best Single Choice

Option A. It gives the highest chance of a good trip with fewer weather, road, and RV constraints.

Operational Notes

  • Plan no-hookup nights in Nevada/Arizona desert sections; use developed campgrounds or towns for dump stations every 2-3 days.
  • Grand Canyon: do not move the RV around all day. Park early, then use shuttles.
  • Oatman/Sitgreaves Pass: paved and feasible for a mid-size Cruise America RV, but slow, twisty, and not a bad-weather road.
  • Sequoia: approach by Highway 180 from Fresno for RV sanity.
  • Yosemite: only choose Option B if Tioga Pass and current conditions cooperate.
